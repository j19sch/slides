speaker notes

Elephant Carpaccio - what?

exercise created by Alistair Cockburn in 2008(?)

focus: really thin vertical slices

a simple retail calculator, three inputs and one input,
any programming language, any interface

break down in 15-20 slices

build in 5 iterations of 8 minutes

Elephant Carpaccio - why?

thinner slices lead to:

- better quality
- sooner feedback
- easier planning
- delivering more often

seems simple, is counter-intuitive

Elephant Carpaccio - how?

Walk-in & Intro (10 mins)

Why slice small? (10 mins)

Backlog breakdown (40 mins)

Short break (10 mins)

Development (45 mins)

Short break (5 mins)

Review (10 mins)

Debrief (20 mins)

2.5 hours instead of 90-120 minutes what is the first slice? & how can you slice the VAT? development: do you need iterations? review: demo to each other, acceptance test "It was the building that crystallized the gap between being able to define small pieces of work, and what it's like to actually build like that." - EZ

How I prepared

read blog posts and the facilitation guide

do the exercise on my own

adapt the exercise to remote

How it went

well and better every time (after some tweaks)

challenges

- runnning short on time
- finding the right level of intervention
- getting the most out of the debrief
ran it three times first slice and slicing techniques in group remote makes debrief harder, because you know less of what happens

What happened after

participants stuck around to discuss more

participants applied it in their teams

Elizabeth Zagroba ran it twice at the same company

friend was surprised to find my blog posts

blog post "two times remote elephant carpaccio" is most viewed one (1k+ since Oct 21)

Three pieces of advice

prep, then prep some more

be ready for different scenarios
your own retro is prep for next time

the debrief is where most of the learning happens

focus should be on reflecting and learning, not on delivering
participants teach themselves and each other

remote is slower and more opaque

so prep and plan accordingly
