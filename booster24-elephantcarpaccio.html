speaker notes

Elephant Carpaccio - what?

Alistair Cockburn, earliest blog post October 2008

really thin vertical slices

a simple retail calculator, three inputs and one input, any programming language, any interface

instead of 2-3 slices => 15-20 slices

5 iterations of 8 minutes

Elephant Carpaccio - why?

better quality

sooner feedback

easier planning

delivering more often

counter-intuitive, e.g. hard-coding stuff

Elephant Carpaccio - how?

Walk-in & Intro (10 mins)
Why slice small? (10 mins)
Backlog breakdown (40 mins)
Short break (10 mins)
Development (45 mins)
Short break (5 mins)
Review (10 mins)
Debrief (20 mins)
2.5 hours instead of 90-120 minutes what is the first slice? & how can you slice the VAT? development: do you need iterations? review: demo to each other, acceptance test "It was the building that crystallized the gap between being able to define small pieces of work, and what it's like to actually build like that." - EZ

How I prepared

How it went

well

I learned to run it better by doing it three times

runnning short on time, more outside workshop

finding the right level of intervention

first slice and slicing techniques in group

getting the most out of the debrief, remote makes that harder, because you know less of what happens

What happened after

I did have a really good conversation (twice) with one of the participants right after the workshop about in which contexts feature slicing would work better or worse.

And one participant mentioned they have started applying it immediately to a new initiative.

team I mostly did this for, kept applying it

EZ facilitated the workshop twice at same company after I had left, made own changes

friend found my posts

prep

prep -> ready for anything
your own retro is prep for next time

experiential workshops

participants teach yourself and each other
not about delivering, about learning, if only learning what not to do
debrief -> where the learning happens
