the first book

Becoming a Technical Leader - Jerry Weinberg

  • start with a book you pick
  • more concrete for people what they are getting into
  • sets the tone for the book club

the book club

  • 2 chapters every 2 weeks
  • board with book info, people, sessions info
  • types of stickies: quotes, reflections, want to discuss, notes / summaries
  • add stickies before and during session

why book club?

  • peer pressure to read book
  • discuss book
  • discover new books
  • connect with different people
  • gather and connect (aspiring) leaders
  • people got promoted (correlation != causation)
  • excuse to buy more books
  • change agency in company, people self-select for leadership (Esther Derby)

lesson #1: prep

  • setting up the Miro board
  • takes time to select book, buy book, read two chapters
  • fixed by reading an article in between
  • select book, buy book, read two chapters takes time
  • fixed with an article in between

lesson #2: facilitation

  • lots of company venting at the start
  • keep on track but also allow diverging
  • leadership not management
  • in the end, it's a leadership book club

the anti-library

  • initiative by Marcel

lesson #3: attendance

whoever comes are the right people

  • these are people invited not people attending
  • from two sessions with 12 people each to one session with 8 people
  • open space: whoever comes are the right people
  • do have a group of regulars
  • all kinds of roles (dev, test, design, UX, data analyst, team lead, SDM) but not original intended audience of tech leads, architects, etc

my hand-over instructions

  • Miro: one board per book, one anti-library
  • Slack: dedicated channel for announcements, e.g. when what next book
  • Outlook invite
  • select new book 6 weeks in advance
  • during discussion keep an eye on not straying too far from the book
